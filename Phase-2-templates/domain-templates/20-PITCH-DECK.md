## 🎯 TEMPLATE

```
ROLE: You are a venture capital advisor and pitch deck expert who has helped startups raise over $500M in funding.
Your specialty: Crafting narratives that capture investor attention in the first 60 seconds and build conviction through the entire deck.

CONTEXT: I'm creating a pitch deck for [COMPANY_NAME], a [STAGE: seed/Series A/etc.] startup.
We need to raise [FUNDING_AMOUNT] to [PRIMARY_USE: product development/market expansion/team growth].
Our traction includes [KEY_METRICS].
Our market is [MARKET_DESCRIPTION].
Our unique advantage is [COMPETITIVE_MOAT].

TASK: Create a complete pitch deck narrative with speaker notes and slide-by-slide content for a [NUMBER_OF_SLIDES]-slide deck.

REQUIREMENTS:
- OPENING (Slide 1): The "hook" — one-sentence that makes investors lean forward
- PROBLEM (Slides 2-3): The pain point, who has it, current (inadequate) solutions
- SOLUTION (Slides 4-5): Your product, demo/screenshot, "aha" moment explanation
- MARKET (Slides 6-7): Size, growth, timing (why now?)
- PRODUCT (Slides 8-9): Features, tech, roadmap, IP
- TRACTION (Slide 10): Metrics, growth rate, customer validation
- BUSINESS MODEL (Slide 11): Revenue streams, pricing, unit economics
- COMPETITION (Slide 12): Landscape matrix, differentiation
- TEAM (Slide 13): Key players, relevant experience, hiring plan
- FINANCIALS (Slide 14): 3-year projection, key assumptions
- THE ASK (Slide 15): Amount, use of funds, milestones
- CLOSING (Slide 16): Vision statement, contact info, appendix teasers

Each slide needs:
- Headline (one compelling sentence)
- 3-5 bullet points (max)
- Visual direction
- Speaker notes (what you actually say)
- Anticipated investor questions + answers

CONSTRAINTS:
- NO "we're the Uber for X" analogies — explain directly
- NEVER bury the lead — start with your strongest point
- NO financial projections without clear assumptions
- NEVER skip "why now" — timing is everything in venture
- No more than 20 words per bullet point
- Each slide supports ONE idea only

AUDIENCE: [INVESTOR_TYPE: angels/VCs/corporate] focused on [SECTOR/STAGE] who typically ask about [THEIR_HOT_BUTTON_ISSUE]

• [Point 3]
Visual: [Specific direction]
Speaker Notes: [Exact words to say]
Anticipated Q&A: [Question] → [Answer]

[Repeat for all slides]

**APPENDIX SLIDES** (backup)
[List of supporting slides for deep-dive questions]
```

## 🎨 REAL EXAMPLES

**Example 1: Opening/Hook Slide**
```
Slide 1: The Future of [Industry]

Headline: "We've reduced [expensive process] from $50K and 6 months to $500 and 48 hours"

Visual: Before/after split screen with time/money metrics

Speaker Notes:
"Every company in [industry] faces this bottleneck. It's killing their speed to market. 
We've built the infrastructure that makes it instant. I'm going to show you how we're 
becoming the operating system for [sector]."

Anticipated Q&A:
"Is that $500 all-in or per unit?" → 
"All-in. That includes our margin. Their previous solution cost them $50K plus 6 months 
of delayed revenue."
```

**Example 2: Traction Slide**
```
Slide 10: Traction & Growth

Headline: "Customers are voting with their wallets"

Bullets:
• $2.3M ARR, growing 40% MoM
• 150+ enterprise customers, 95% retention
• $15K average contract value, up 50% from 6 months ago
• Featured in [Publication], customer waitlist of 500+
• Break-even cash flow achieved last quarter

Visual: Up-and-to-right graph with key milestones marked

Speaker Notes:
"We've found product-market fit. The question isn't 'will this work?'—it's 'how fast 
can we scale it?' That's why we're raising now."

Anticipated Q&A:
"What's driving that retention rate?" →
"Two things: First, we integrate deeply into their workflow—high switching costs. 
Second, our NPS is 72, so they genuinely love the product."
```

## 🔧 VARIATIONS

**Variation 1: Demo-Heavy Deck (Product/Tech focus)**
```
Emphasis: Live demo or video in slides 4-5
Add: Technical architecture slide, security/compliance slide
Reduce: Market sizing (assume investors know it)
Timing: 10 minutes presentation + 20 minutes demo + 20 minutes Q&A
```

**Variation 2: Traction-First Deck (Later stage)**
```
Emphasis: Lead with metrics (revenue, growth, efficiency)
Add: Expansion metrics, competitive win rates, team scaling
Reduce: Problem explanation (shorter—market is proven)
Tone: "We're scaling something that works" vs. "we think this might work"
```

**Variation 3: Vision-First Deck (Pre-seed/Concept)**
```
Emphasis: Team credentials, market opportunity, product vision
Add: Technical alpha, letters of intent from customers
Reduce: Financial projections (too early)
Substitute: Traction slide becomes "Validation" (LOIs, waitlist, expert endorsements)
```

## ✅ QUALITY CHECKLIST

- [ ] First slide makes investors lean forward in 10 seconds
- [ ] Problem is a "hair-on-fire" pain (not nice-to-have)
- [ ] Solution demo/visual creates "aha" moment
- [ ] Market size is bottom-up validated (not just top-down "1% of $1T")
- [ ] "Why now" timing is clearly articulated
- [ ] Traction metrics are specific and trending up
- [ ] Unit economics show path to profitability
- [ ] Competitive matrix shows clear differentiation
- [ ] Team slide proves domain expertise
- [ ] The Ask is specific with milestones
- [ ] Every slide passes the "so what?" test

## 💡 PRO TIPS

- **The 10/20/30 rule**: 10 slides, 20 minutes, 30-point font minimum
- **One idea per slide**: If you have two ideas, make two slides
- **Lead with traction if you have it**: Nothing beats "we're already growing fast"
- **The "cocktail party test"**: Can someone explain your business after hearing your pitch?
- **Know your kryptonite**: Identify the one slide investors will challenge most, prepare extra backup
- **Story arc over data dump**: Make them feel the problem before showing the solution
- **End with momentum**: Last slide should leave them wanting more, not feeling complete

## 🔄 ITERATION WORKFLOW

1. **Outline**: Structure the narrative arc before any content
2. **Draft**: Write headlines and bullets for all slides
3. **Visual pass**: Add specific visual direction (not "graph here")
4. **Rehearsal**: Practice with timer — 20 minutes max
5. **Friendly pitch**: Present to mentors/advisors for feedback
6. **Investor pitch**: Start with lower-priority investors to refine
7. **Iterate**: Update traction metrics monthly, refresh quarterly
