**Example 1: Career Decision (Job Offer)**
```

OPTIONS:
1. Accept Offer (Company B)

3. Work-Life Balance (20%) — Hours, flexibility, commute, culture
4. Stability (15%) — Company health, runway, market position
5. Mission Alignment (15%) — Do I care about what they do?

Pros:
• 40% higher base salary, better equity upside
• Fast-growing team with clear promotion path
• Remote-first policy
Cons:
• Earlier stage company (higher risk)
• Longer hours expected
• New industry (steeper learning curve)
Score: 82/100

DECISION TESTS:
10-10-10: Excited now, probably grateful in 10 months, career-defining in 10 years
Regret Test: Would regret NOT taking the growth opportunity
Values: Aligns with my value of continuous growth over comfort

RECOMMENDATION: Accept Offer (Option 1)
Contingency: Negotiate 6-month review clause in case of misalignment
```

## 🔧 VARIATIONS

**Variation 1: Rapid Decision (Under 1 Hour)**
```
Simplified framework:
- Define decision (5 min)
- List 3 options (5 min)
- Identify 3 key criteria (5 min)
- Score quickly (10 min)
- 10-10-10 test (5 min)
- Decide and act (remaining time)
Remove: Detailed pro/con, risk matrix, assumption audit
```

**Variation 2: Group/Team Decision**
```
Add: Stakeholder input gathering, facilitated discussion guide, consensus-building process
Emphasis: Decision rights (who decides vs. who advises), voting mechanisms
Remove: Personal values alignment (replace with organizational values)
```

**Variation 3: High-Stakes Irreversible Decision**
```
Add: Pre-mortem (imagine it failed, work backwards), red team review, second opinion from trusted advisor
Emphasis: Information gathering phase, optionality preservation
Timeline: Extend analysis period, set firm decision date
```

## ✅ QUALITY CHECKLIST

- [ ] Decision is clearly defined (not vague like "should I change jobs?")
- [ ] At least 3 viable options generated (not just A vs. B)
- [ ] Criteria are weighted (not all equally important)
- [ ] "Do nothing" option analyzed as baseline
- [ ] Sunk costs acknowledged but not driving decision
- [ ] 10-10-10 test performed
- [ ] Regret minimization test performed
- [ ] Key assumptions identified and validated
- [ ] Top risks have mitigation strategies
- [ ] Clear recommendation with confidence level
- [ ] Immediate action steps specified
- [ ] Decision deadline is set (prevents paralysis)

## 💡 PRO TIPS

- **The 40-70 rule**: Make decisions with 40-70% of the information — waiting for 100% is paralysis, less than 40% is guessing
- **Pre-mortem technique**: "It's one year from now and this decision failed catastrophically — why?" reveals hidden risks
- **Separate deciding from doing**: Make the decision, then execute with full commitment
- **The "hell yeah or no" test**: If it's not a clear yes, it's probably a no
- **Values as filter**: When options seem close, values alignment breaks ties
- **Reversible decisions fast, irreversible decisions slow**: Match analysis depth to decision reversibility
- **Document your reasoning**: You'll learn from reviewing past decisions

## 🔄 ITERATION WORKFLOW

1. **Clarify**: Define exactly what decision needs to be made
2. **Generate**: Brainstorm 4-6 viable options (including non-obvious)
3. **Criteria**: Identify 5-8 weighted factors
4. **Analyze**: Score each option, identify pros/cons
5. **Test**: Apply 10-10-10, regret minimization, values alignment
6. **Validate**: Check assumptions, assess risks
7. **Decide**: Make the call with confidence level
8. **Execute**: Take immediate action (commitment matters more than perfect analysis)
9. **Review**: After 3-6 months, review decision quality to improve future choices
