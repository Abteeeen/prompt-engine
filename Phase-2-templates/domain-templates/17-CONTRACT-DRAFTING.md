## 🎯 TEMPLATE

```
ROLE: You are a seasoned corporate attorney with 20+ years drafting contracts for startups, enterprises, and high-stakes transactions.
Your specialty: Creating clear, enforceable contract language that protects all parties while enabling business.

CONTEXT: I need to draft a [CONTRACT_TYPE: service agreement/employment/NDA/partnership/license] contract for [SITUATION: vendor relationship/employee hire/joint venture].
My business: [YOUR_BUSINESS_TYPE] providing [SERVICE/PRODUCT].
Counterparty: [COUNTERPARTY_TYPE: individual/company/government].

TASK: Create a comprehensive contract template with key clauses, defined terms, and risk mitigation language.

REQUIREMENTS:
- PREAMBLE: Clear identification of parties, recitals, and effective date
- DEFINITIONS SECTION: 5-8 key terms precisely defined
- CORE OBLIGATIONS: What each party must do, with specificity
- PAYMENT TERMS: Schedule, milestones, late fees, currency
- INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY: Ownership, licenses, work-for-hire provisions
- CONFIDENTIALITY: Scope, duration, exceptions, return of materials
- REPRESENTATIONS & WARRANTIES: Each party's guarantees
- LIMITATION OF LIABILITY: Cap on damages, excluded liabilities
- TERMINATION: For cause, for convenience, notice periods
- DISPUTE RESOLUTION: Governing law, venue, arbitration vs. litigation
- GENERAL PROVISIONS: Severability, amendment, assignment, notices
- SIGNATURE BLOCKS: Proper execution format

CONSTRAINTS:
- NO legalese for its own sake — clear language over archaic terms
- NEVER include clauses you don't understand or can't explain
- NO unlimited liability or "blank check" indemnification
- NEVER skip governing law and venue selection
- No vague performance standards — metrics must be measurable

AUDIENCE: [COUNTERPARTY_TYPE] with [LEGAL_SOPHISTICATION: high/moderate/none] who needs [PROTECTION_LEVEL: maximum/balanced/minimal]

OUTPUT FORMAT:
**CONTRACT TITLE**: [Type] Agreement

**PARTIES**:
- Party A: [Your entity name and role]
- Party B: [Counterparty name and role]

**RECITALS** (WHEREAS clauses):
[Business context for the agreement]

**ARTICLE 1: DEFINITIONS**
[5-8 defined terms]

**ARTICLE 2: [PRIMARY SUBJECT MATTER]**
[Core obligations]

[Continue through all articles]

**SIGNATURE PAGE**
[Proper execution block]

**SELF-CHECK BEFORE RESPONDING:**
Rate your contract:
- Clarity for non-lawyers (1-3):
- Risk mitigation (1-3):
- Enforceability (1-3):
If total < 7 — simplify language and add specificity to obligations.
```

## 🎨 REAL EXAMPLES

**Example 1: Service Agreement (Consulting)**
```
ARTICLE 3: SCOPE OF SERVICES

3.1 Services. Consultant shall provide [specific services] as described in Exhibit A attached hereto.

3.2 Deliverables. Consultant shall deliver: [list with due dates]
(a) [Deliverable 1] by [Date]
(b) [Deliverable 2] by [Date]

3.3 Acceptance. Client shall review Deliverables within [5] business days. 
Deliverables are deemed accepted unless Client provides written rejection with specific deficiencies.
```

**Example 2: Mutual NDA**
```
ARTICLE 2: DEFINITIONS

2.1 "Confidential Information" means any and all non-public information disclosed by either party, 
whether oral, written, or electronic, that is marked or identified as confidential at the time of 
disclosure or that a reasonable person would consider confidential given the nature of the information 
or circumstances of disclosure.

2.2 Exceptions. Confidential Information does not include information that: 
(a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of Recipient;
(b) was rightfully in Recipient's possession prior to disclosure;
(c) is rightfully obtained from a third party without restriction; or
(d) is independently developed without use of Discloser's Confidential Information.
```

- Open source disclosures

```
Additional Clauses:
- Capital contributions

- Decision-making and voting

- Non-compete during and after

```

## ✅ QUALITY CHECKLIST

- [ ] Parties identified with full legal names and addresses
- [ ] Key terms defined (don't use undefined jargon)
- [ ] Obligations are specific and measurable
- [ ] Payment terms include amount, schedule, and late penalties
- [ ] IP ownership is explicitly stated (who owns what)
- [ ] Confidentiality covers what, for how long, and exceptions
- [ ] Liability is capped at reasonable amount (not unlimited)
- [ ] Termination rights are clear (for cause vs. convenience)
- [ ] Dispute resolution specifies law, venue, and mechanism
- [ ] Signature blocks include date and title

## 💡 PRO TIPS

- **Start with the business deal**: Write down the commercial terms in plain English first, then translate to legalese
- **Use "shall" for obligations, "may" for permissions, "will" for future events**: Precise language prevents disputes
- **Cross-reference carefully**: If Article 3 references Exhibit A, ensure Exhibit A exists
- **Define undefined capitalized terms**: First time a term appears, it should be in quotes and defined
- **Severability clause is essential**: If one provision is unenforceable, the rest survives
- **Have a second pair of eyes review**: Legal documents need proofreading more than most

## 🔄 ITERATION WORKFLOW

1. **Business terms first**: List all commercial points before drafting
2. **Template selection**: Start with similar existing contract
3. **Clause drafting**: Write each article with specificity
4. **Cross-check**: Ensure defined terms used consistently
5. **Counterparty review**: Expect pushback on liability and IP clauses
6. **Legal review**: Have attorney review before execution
7. **Post-execution**: Store with clear naming, set calendar reminders
