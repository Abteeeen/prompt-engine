**Effectiveness**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Essential for team development)

## 🎯 TEMPLATE

```
ROLE: You are an HR leader and performance coach who has guided thousands of managers through effective performance conversations.
Your specialty: Creating review frameworks that balance accountability with growth and leave employees motivated, not defensive.

CONTEXT: I'm conducting a [REVIEW_TYPE: annual/quarterly/30-60-90] performance review for [EMPLOYEE_NAME].
Role: [EMPLOYEE_ROLE], [TENURE] with the company.
Performance level: [LEVEL: exceeds/meets/needs development].
Key focus areas: [GROWTH_AREAS].
Career goals: [EMPLOYEE_ASPIRATIONS].
Team context: [TEAM_DYNAMICS].

TASK: Create a structured performance review with balanced feedback, specific examples, and forward-looking development plan.

REQUIREMENTS:
- SELF-ASSESSMENT INTEGRATION: Reference their input, address gaps diplomatically
- ACCOMPLISHMENTS: 3-5 specific wins with impact metrics
- GROWTH AREAS: 2-3 development opportunities framed as growth, not failure
- BEHAVIORAL EXAMPLES: Specific situations, not general traits (SBI model)
- GOAL ALIGNMENT: Connect individual goals to team/company objectives
- DEVELOPMENT PLAN: Concrete actions, resources, timeline, success metrics
- CAREER PATH: Next role or skill progression discussion
- FEEDBACK EXCHANGE: Questions to solicit their perspective
- MANAGER COMMITMENTS: What you'll do to support their growth
- DOCUMENTATION: Clear summary for HR file and future reference

CONSTRAINTS:
- NO surprise feedback — reviews summarize known performance, not introduce new issues
- NEVER label the person ("you're not strategic") — focus on behaviors and outcomes
- NO recency bias — cover the full period, not just last month
- NEVER compare to other employees — focus on their own trajectory
- Don't overload growth areas — 2-3 max, with clear priority
- No vague development plans — specific actions with dates
- Reviews are forward-looking, not just rear-view mirrors

AUDIENCE: [EMPLOYEE_TYPE: high performer/solid contributor/emerging talent] with [CAREER_STAGE] experience who responds best to [FEEDBACK_STYLE: direct/supportive/data-driven]

OUTPUT FORMAT:
**PERFORMANCE REVIEW: [EMPLOYEE_NAME]**
Review Period: [DATES]
Review Type: [Annual/Quarterly/Probationary]
Manager: [YOUR_NAME]
Date: [DATE]

---

**1. REVIEW OPENING**
[Frame the conversation: appreciation for their work, review purpose, structure]

Example: "Thank you for your contributions this [period]. This review covers what you've accomplished, where you're growing, and where we're headed together."

**2. SELF-ASSESSMENT SYNTHESIS**
Employee's view: [Summary of their self-assessment]

Alignment/Divergence:
• Areas of agreement: [Where you see the same strengths/challenges]
• Areas to discuss: [Where perspectives differ — address diplomatically]

**3. KEY ACCOMPLISHMENTS** (What went well)

Accomplishment 1: [Specific outcome]
• Context: [Situation]
• Your actions: [What they did]
• Impact: [Measurable result]
• Skills demonstrated: [Competencies shown]

[Repeat for 3-5 accomplishments]

**4. GROWTH & DEVELOPMENT AREAS** (Where to grow)

Area 1: [Specific competency or behavior]
• Examples: [SBI: Situation-Behavior-Impact]
  - Situation: [When/where]
  - Behavior: [What was observed]
  - Impact: [Effect on team/results]
• Why it matters: [Connection to role/team/company goals]
• Growth path: [Specific development action]

[Repeat for 2-3 areas]

**5. GOAL ALIGNMENT**
Employee's goals: [What they want to achieve]

Company/team needs: [What the organization needs]

Alignment: [How their growth serves both]

**6. DEVELOPMENT PLAN** (Forward-looking)

Next 30 days:
• [Specific action] — Success metric: [How you'll know]

Next 90 days:
• [Specific action] — Success metric: [How you'll know]

Next 6-12 months:
• [Specific action] — Success metric: [How you'll know]

Resources/support:
• [Training/mentoring/projects you'll provide]

**7. CAREER PROGRESSION**
Current trajectory: [On track / Excelling / Needs support]

Next role/skills:
• [What they're preparing for]
• [Gap analysis: what's needed]
• [Timeline estimate]

**8. MANAGER COMMITMENTS**
What I'll do to support your growth:
• [Specific action 1 — by when]
• [Specific action 2 — by when]

**9. EMPLOYEE INPUT & QUESTIONS**
Questions to ask them:
• [Open-ended question about their experience]
• [Question about support needed]
• [Question about career interests]

**10. REVIEW SUMMARY & NEXT STEPS**
Overall rating: [If applicable]
Key themes: [One-paragraph synthesis]
Next check-in: [When you'll follow up]
Signatures: [Employee and manager acknowledgment]

---

**SELF-CHECK BEFORE RESPONDING:**
Rate your review:
- Balance (1-3): Is it fair and balanced?
- Specificity (1-3): Are there concrete examples?
- Actionability (1-3): Is the path forward clear?
If total < 7 — add more examples or clarify the development plan.
```

## 🎨 REAL EXAMPLES

**Example 1: Strong Performer Growth Conversation**
```
4. GROWTH & DEVELOPMENT AREAS

Area: Strategic Influence
Examples:
• Situation: Q3 product roadmap decision
• Behavior: You had strong opinions but presented them primarily 1:1 with me rather than in the cross-functional forum
• Impact: Your insights didn't influence the decision as much as they could have — the final plan missed some technical constraints you had identified

Why it matters: As you grow toward senior roles, your impact scales through influencing others, not just your own excellent execution.

Growth path: 
• Practice presenting recommendations to broader groups
• Frame suggestions in terms of shared goals, not just technical correctness
• I'll sponsor you for our "Influencing Without Authority" workshop next quarter
```

**Example 2: Development Needed (Delicate)**
```
4. GROWTH & DEVELOPMENT AREAS

Area: Meeting Commitments
Examples:
• Situation: Sprint planning over past 3 sprints
• Behavior: Committed to 8 story points, delivered 4-5 consistently
• Impact: Team had to replan mid-sprint twice, causing scope cuts and stakeholder disappointment

Why it matters: Reliability builds trust. When commitments slip, it affects the entire team's planning and credibility.

Growth path:
• We'll pair on estimation for next 2 sprints — I'll help you calibrate
• Break large stories down more granularly
• Flag blockers within 24 hours, not at standup
• Check-in: Weekly 1:1s for next month to build this muscle

Note: I believe you can absolutely develop here — I've seen you deliver when scope is clear. Let's build that consistency.
```

## 🔧 VARIATIONS

**Variation 1: 30-60-90 Day Review (New Hire)**
```
Focus: Onboarding success, early wins, gap identification
Structure:
• Learning progress: What they've mastered
• Relationship building: Connections made
• Quick wins: Early contributions
• Support needs: What they need to succeed
• 90-day goals: What success looks like going forward
Tone: Supportive, clarifying expectations, building relationship
```

**Variation 2: High Performer Retention Conversation**
```
Focus: Career acceleration, new challenges, recognition
Structure:
• Impact amplification: How their work scaled
• New opportunities: Stretch assignments, visibility
• Career path: Fast-track options, next role prep
• Recognition: Formal and informal appreciation
• Retention check: What would keep them engaged long-term
Tone: Celebratory, investment-focused, partnership
```

**Variation 3: Performance Improvement Plan**
```
Focus: Clear expectations, support structure, consequences
Structure:
• Gap clarity: Specific behaviors/outcomes needing change
• Timeline: Improvement period with check-ins
• Support provided: Resources, coaching, reduced scope if needed
• Success criteria: Measurable standards for continuation
• Consequences: What happens if improvement doesn't occur
Tone: Direct, supportive but clear, fair process
```

## ✅ QUALITY CHECKLIST

- [ ] No surprise feedback — all points previously discussed
- [ ] Accomplishments have specific examples and impact metrics
- [ ] Growth areas use SBI (Situation-Behavior-Impact) model
- [ ] Development plan has specific actions, timelines, and metrics
- [ ] Career path discussion included
- [ ] Manager commitments are concrete and dated
- [ ] Employee input solicited through open questions
- [ ] Forward-looking, not just retrospective
- [ ] Balanced — neither overly critical nor inflated praise
- [ ] Documented clearly for HR and future reference

## 💡 PRO TIPS

- **SBI model for constructive feedback**: Situation (when/where) → Behavior (what you saw) → Impact (result)
- **The "what/why/how" framework**: What happened → Why it matters → How to grow
- **Balance 3:1**: Three positive observations for every growth area
- **Separate performance from potential**: Someone can perform well but not be ready for next level
- **Document in real-time**: Don't rely on memory — note accomplishments and feedback as they happen
- **The growth mindset frame**: "Not yet" instead of "can't" — skills are developable
- **Follow up**: The review is the start, not the end — check in on development plans

## 🔄 ITERATION WORKFLOW

1. **Preparation**: Gather data, self-assessment, peer feedback
2. **Draft**: Write using framework, include specific examples
3. **Calibration**: Review with HR/peer managers for consistency
4. **Delivery**: Schedule uninterrupted time, create safe space
5. **Dialogue**: Two-way conversation, not monologue
6. **Documentation**: Finalize written review, get signatures
7. **Follow-through**: Execute development plan, schedule check-ins
8. **Review the review**: What worked? What didn't? Improve next time
