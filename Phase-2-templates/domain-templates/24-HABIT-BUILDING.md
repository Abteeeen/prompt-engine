## 🎨 REAL EXAMPLES

**Example 1: Exercise Habit**
```
HABIT SPECIFICATION:
Tiny start: Put on running shoes and step outside (2 min max)
Full target: Run 5K every morning
Frequency: Daily (6 days/week with 1 rest day)

IDENTITY:
"I am a runner." 
Evidence: I put on my shoes every morning. Runners run, but runners also just put on shoes.

ENVIRONMENT:
• Shoes by bed (visible, easy)
• Alarm labeled "Become a runner" (identity cue)
• Running clothes laid out night before (friction removal)
• Coffee maker auto-brews at 6am (reward waiting)

IMPLEMENTATION INTENTION:
"When my 6am alarm goes off, I will put on my running shoes and step outside."

TEMPTATION BUNDLING:
"I only listen to my favorite podcast while running."

TRACKING:
• Calendar on wall — big X every day I put on shoes
• Strava for actual runs (when they happen naturally)
• Text accountability partner by 7am: "Shoes on 👟"

PROGRESSION:
Week 1-2: Just shoes + outside (stand there if needed)
Week 3-4: Walk to corner and back
Week 5-6: Run 1K
Week 7+: Build to 5K naturally as desire increases

OBSTACLES:
"It's raining" → Run on treadmill or just step outside and decide
"I'm sore" → Just put on shoes (habit maintenance over performance)
"I woke up late" → 2-minute version still counts

RELAPSE PROTOCOL:
Missed a day? → Shoes on immediately when I remember. Never miss twice.
Missed three days? → Return to week 1 protocol for 3 days.
Self-talk: "One missed day is data, not destiny."
```

**Example 2: Writing Habit**
```
HABIT SPECIFICATION:
Tiny start: Open laptop and write one sentence (2 min)
Full target: Write 1000 words daily

IDENTITY:
"I am a writer. Writers write, even just one sentence."

ENVIRONMENT:
• Laptop always on desk (never put away)
• Distraction blockers (Freedom app, phone in another room)
• Same coffee shop corner table (context cue)
• Noise-canceling headphones + writing playlist (sensory anchor)

IMPLEMENTATION INTENTION:
"When I finish my morning coffee, I will open my writing document and write one sentence."

ACCOUNTABILITY:
• Twitter thread: "Day X of writing daily" (public commitment)
• Writing group weekly check-in
• Goal: 30-day streak, then 100-day

OBSTACLES:
"I don't know what to write" → Write "I don't know what to write today but..." and continue
"I don't have time" → One sentence takes 30 seconds
"It's terrible quality" → First drafts are allowed to be terrible. Editing is separate.
```

## 🔧 VARIATIONS

**Variation 1: Elimination Habit (Stop Doing)**
```
Focus: Remove unwanted behavior
Add: Replacement behavior, friction increase, cue analysis
Remove: Progression (reverse — start with full reduction)
Emphasis: Environment design over willpower, identity shift from "trying to quit" to "not a smoker/drinker/etc."
```

**Variation 2: Social/Relationship Habit**
```
Focus: Connecting with others regularly
Add: Scheduling mechanisms, conversation prompts, social accountability
Emphasis: Reciprocity, making it easy for others too
Examples: Weekly check-ins with friends, daily gratitude expression, monthly date nights
```

**Variation 3: Learning/Skill Habit**
```
Focus: Consistent practice and improvement
Add: Deliberate practice structure, feedback loops, skill progression
Emphasis: Minimum effective dose (quality over quantity)
Examples: Language practice, instrument playing, coding exercises
```

- [ ] Accountability is scheduled (not just hoped for)

- [ ] Celebration/reward is defined

## 💡 PRO TIPS

- **The 2-minute rule**: Any habit can start with a 2-minute version — the goal is to master showing up
- **Habit stacking**: Attach new habit to existing automatic habit (after coffee, before shower)
- **Design for the lazy version**: Your future tired self is who you're planning for
- **Consistency > intensity**: 5 minutes every day beats 2 hours once a week
- **Never miss twice**: One miss is a data point, two is a pattern — resume immediately
- **Track don't judge**: Data helps, shame doesn't — just mark whether you did it or not
- **Identity first**: Ask "what would a [runner/writer/meditator] do?" and do that

## 🔄 ITERATION WORKFLOW

1. **Week 1**: Focus entirely on showing up (quality irrelevant)
2. **Week 2**: Build consistency, adjust environment if failing
3. **Week 3**: Gradual expansion if 90%+ adherence
4. **Week 4**: Lock in the standard version
5. **Month 2**: Continue with possible progression
6. **Monthly review**: Is it automatic? Does it need adjustment?
7. **Ongoing**: Same habit for 6+ months before adding new major habits
