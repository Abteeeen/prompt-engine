REQUIREMENTS:
- BRAND ESSENCE: Core identity in 2-3 words + supporting narrative
- BRAND PERSONALITY: 3-5 human traits with definitions and expressions
- VOICE ATTRIBUTES: How we sound (with do/don't examples)
- TONE SPECTRUM: How tone shifts across contexts (marketing vs. support vs. legal)
- MESSAGING PILLARS: 3-4 core themes that underpin all communication

- NO generic descriptors ("we're innovative and customer-focused") — everyone says that
- NEVER copy competitor voices — differentiation is the goal
- NO voice without examples — abstract principles don't help writers
- NEVER be exclusionary or offensive in the name of "edgy"
- Don't create rules you can't explain why they exist
- Voice must be sustainable — can your team actually write this way daily?
- Must work across all channels — if it only works on Instagram, it's not brand voice

## 🎨 REAL EXAMPLES

**Example 1: SaaS Startup (Friendly Expert)**
```
BRAND ESSENCE: "Approachable Expertise"
We make complex things simple without talking down. We're the friend who's really good at this stuff and genuinely wants to help.

PERSONALITY TRAITS:
1. Knowledgeable — We know our stuff deeply
   Sounds like: "Here's what works (and what doesn't)..."
   Never: "As everyone knows..." or overusing jargon

2. Friendly — We like our customers and show it
   Sounds like: "You're going to love this feature..."
   Never: Fake cheerfulness or overly formal

3. Direct — We get to the point
   Sounds like: "This takes 5 minutes. Here's how:"
   Never: Beating around the bush or corporate fluff

TONE SPECTRUM:
Marketing: Enthusiastic — "Finally, a solution that actually works"
Support: Empathetic — "I understand why that's frustrating. Let's fix it."
Product: Clear — "Click here to [specific action]"
Legal: Straightforward — No attempts to be "friendly" in contracts

MESSAGING PILLAR: "Complexity Simplified"
Key message: "You shouldn't need a PhD to [task]"
Proof point: "Our customers save 10 hours/week on average"
```

**Example 2: Luxury Brand (Refined Excellence)**
```
BRAND ESSENCE: "Quiet Confidence"
We don't need to shout. Our quality speaks. We're for people who appreciate the details others miss.

PERSONALITY TRAITS:
1. Refined — Polished without being pretentious
2. Confident — We stand behind what we make
3. Thoughtful — Every detail considered

VOICE ATTRIBUTES:
Confident not boastful:
✅ "Crafted to last a lifetime"
❌ "The best product you'll ever buy"

Specific not vague:
✅ "18-gauge copper hand-hammered by artisans"
❌ "Premium quality materials"

TONE: Never urgent, never discount-focused. Calm assurance.
```

## 🔧 VARIATIONS

**Variation 1: Technical/Developer Brand**
```
Focus: Precision, capability, respect for technical depth
Tone: Smart, efficient, no marketing fluff
Considerations:
• Use correct technical terminology
• Show, don't tell (code examples, technical details)
• Respect developer time (get to the point)
• Acknowledge trade-offs honestly
```

**Variation 2: Consumer Lifestyle Brand**
```
Focus: Aspiration, community, emotional connection
Tone: Relatable, inspiring, inclusive
Considerations:
• Storytelling over specifications
• User-generated content integration
• Cultural sensitivity and inclusivity
• Lifestyle positioning over product features
```

**Variation 3: B2B Enterprise Brand**
```
Focus: Trust, reliability, ROI, partnership
Tone: Professional, confident, consultative
Considerations:
• Risk mitigation language
• Business outcome focus
• Credibility markers (data, case studies)
• Long-term relationship emphasis
```

## 💡 PRO TIPS

- **Start with "we are" not "we are not"**: Define what you are, then note exceptions
- **The competitor test**: If a competitor could claim the same voice, go deeper
- **The founder test**: Would the founder speak this way naturally?
- **Sustainability check**: Can your team write in this voice when tired/busy?
- **Show, don't tell**: Every principle needs an example
- **Review regularly**: Voice evolves — refresh annually
- **Train, don't just document**: Workshops beat PDFs for adoption

## 🔄 ITERATION WORKFLOW

1. **Discovery**: Audit current voice, interview stakeholders, analyze competitors
2. **Framework**: Define essence, personality, attributes
3. **Examples**: Create before/after transformations
4. **Review**: Test with writers and target audience
5. **Refine**: Adjust based on what's not working
6. **Launch**: Train team, distribute guide
7. **Maintenance**: Quarterly spot-checks, annual full review
8. **Evolution**: Refresh as brand matures or pivots
