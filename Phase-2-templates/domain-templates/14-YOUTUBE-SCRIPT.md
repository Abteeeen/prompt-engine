## 🎯 TEMPLATE

```
ROLE: You are an elite YouTube scriptwriter who has written for top creators with millions of subscribers.
Your specialty: Creating scripts that hook viewers in 5 seconds and keep them watching until the CTA.

CONTEXT: I'm creating a [VIDEO_TYPE] YouTube video for my channel about [NICHE/TOPIC].
Target audience: [VIEWER_DEMOGRAPHIC] who want [VIEWER_GOAL].
My unique angle: [DIFFERENTIATOR].

TASK: Write a complete YouTube script including hook, body, and call-to-action optimized for [TARGET_WATCH_TIME] watch time.

REQUIREMENTS:
- HOOK (0-30 sec): Pattern interrupt + stakes establishment + curiosity gap
- INTRO (30-60 sec): Channel intro + topic preview + "what you'll learn" promise
- BODY (3-7 sections): Each with mini-hook + value + transition to next section
- VISUAL CUES: Specific B-roll suggestions, text overlays, and cut points marked [VISUAL: ...]
- CTA (30-60 sec): Soft ask → engagement ask → subscription ask with reason
- WORD COUNT: [TARGET_LENGTH] minutes = approximately [WORD_COUNT] words
- RETENTION TACTICS: Open loops, curiosity gaps, "but first" transitions

CONSTRAINTS:
- NO generic intros like "Hey guys, welcome back to my channel"
- NO asking viewers to "smash that like button" in first 2 minutes
- NEVER say "as you can see" or "basically" or fillers
- NO reading off bullet points — conversational storytelling only
- Maximum 2 minutes per section before visual/pace change

AUDIENCE: [DEMOGRAPHIC] with [EXPERTISE_LEVEL: beginner/intermediate/advanced] knowledge of [TOPIC]

OUTPUT FORMAT:
**TITLE OPTIONS** (3 clickable options under 60 characters)

**THUMBNAIL CONCEPT**: One-sentence visual description

**HOOK** (0-30 sec)
[Script with visual cues]

**SECTION 1: [NAME]** (timestamp)
[Script with visual cues]
[Retention tactic used]

[Continue for all sections]

**CTA SEQUENCE** (end)
[Three-tier ask]

**SEO DESCRIPTION** (150 words max)

SELF-CHECK BEFORE RESPONDING:
Rate your script:
- Hook strength (1-3):
- Information density (1-3):
- CTA persuasion (1-3):
If total < 7 — add more pattern interrupts and tighten the hook.
```

## 🎨 REAL EXAMPLES

**Example 1: Tutorial Video**
```
HOOK:
"I spent $10,000 on Facebook ads so you don't have to. [pause] Here's the exact campaign that finally worked — and the 3 settings that were burning my budget without me knowing."

SECTION 1: The $10K Mistake (0:30-2:00)
"First campaign, I thought more targeting = better results. [Visual: Screen recording of over-complicated audience setup] I had 47 different interest targets. [Beat] Zero sales. [Visual: Empty dashboard] Then I learned about..."
[Retention: Open loop — what did they learn?]

CTA:
"If you want the exact template I use now, it's linked below. Drop a comment with your biggest ad struggle — I'll reply to every one. Subscribe if you want part 2 where I break down retargeting."
```

**Example 2: Storytime Video**
```
HOOK:
"I got fired on a Tuesday. [pause] By Friday, I had 3 job offers paying 40% more. [pause] Here's what I did differently — and why getting fired was the best thing for my career."

BODY STRUCTURE:
Section 1: The firing (setup)
Section 2: The 48-hour strategy (inciting incident)
Section 3: The LinkedIn hack nobody uses (rising action)
Section 4: The offers (climax)
Section 5: What I'd do differently (resolution + lesson)
```

## 🔧 VARIATIONS

**Variation 1: Review Video**
```
Hook: "I've used [Product] for [Time]. [pause] Here's what [Company] doesn't tell you in their marketing."
Structure: Feature test → Real use case → Honest pros/cons → Who it's for/not for
CTA: Alternative recommendation for those it's not for (builds trust)
```

**Variation 2: Vlog/Behind-the-Scenes**
```
Hook: Day-in-the-life moment that creates immediate curiosity
Structure: Chronological but with "meanwhile" cuts for parallel action
Retention: Tease something exciting happening later in the day
```

**Variation 3: Educational/Explainer**
```
Hook: Counter-intuitive statement that challenges common belief
Structure: Myth → Reality → Proof → Application → Mistake to avoid
Visuals: Heavy use of graphics, animations, B-roll
```

## ✅ QUALITY CHECKLIST

- [ ] Hook happens in first 5 seconds (not 30 seconds of intro)
- [ ] Every 30 seconds has a new visual or pace change
- [ ] At least 3 open loops created and resolved by end
- [ ] Each section has clear "what's in it for me" for viewer
- [ ] B-roll/visual cues are specific (not "add graphics here")
- [ ] CTA has 3 distinct asks with clear value exchange
- [ ] Title options are under 60 characters and curiosity-driven

---

## 💡 PRO TIPS

- **Start with the most intense moment**: You can use " rewind" technique to show how you got there
- **The "But First" transition**: "I'll show you the exact template, but first you need to understand..." keeps them watching
- **Visual variety every 30 seconds**: Talking head → screen share → B-roll → graphic → talking head
- **Script for the skip button**: Assume they'll skip at 30, 60, 90 seconds — have mini-hooks there
- **Energy arc**: Start at 9/10 energy, dip to 6/10 for serious content, peak at 10/10 for CTA
- **Read your script out loud**: If you stumble, rewrite. Conversational ≠ unscripted.

## 🔄 ITERATION WORKFLOW

1. **Draft 1**: Write full script, don't edit
2. **Draft 2**: Cut 30% — every sentence must earn its place
3. **Read-through**: Time yourself, mark where energy drops
4. **Visual pass**: Add specific B-roll cues where talking head exceeds 45 seconds
5. **Retention check**: Identify 3 points where viewers might click away — add pattern interrupts
6. **Post-publish**: Analyze retention graph, note drop-off points for next script
