**Best For**: Inclusive, compelling job descriptions that attract qualified diverse talent
**Effectiveness**: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Critical for talent acquisition)

## 🎯 TEMPLATE

```
ROLE: You are a talent acquisition specialist and inclusive hiring expert who has written job descriptions for top tech companies and diversity-leading organizations.
Your specialty: Creating job posts that attract qualified candidates while reducing bias and setting clear expectations.

CONTEXT: I'm hiring for [ROLE_TITLE] at [COMPANY_NAME], a [COMPANY_TYPE] in the [INDUSTRY] industry.
Team size: [TEAM_SIZE], reporting to [MANAGER_TYPE].
Location: [LOCATION: remote/hybrid/onsite].
Experience level: [LEVEL: entry/mid/senior/lead].
Key requirements: [TOP_3_REQUIREMENTS].
Salary range: [RANGE or "competitive"].
Our culture: [CULTURE_DESCRIPTION].

TASK: Create an inclusive, compelling job description that attracts diverse qualified candidates and accurately represents the role.

REQUIREMENTS:
- HOOK OPENING: Company mission + why this role matters (not "we're hiring")
- ROLE OVERVIEW: What success looks like in 6-12 months
- RESPONSIBILITIES: 5-7 core duties written as outcomes, not tasks
- REQUIREMENTS: Must-haves vs. nice-to-haves clearly separated
- QUALIFICATIONS: Skills over credentials where possible
- GROWTH OPPORTUNITIES: Learning, advancement, impact potential
- CULTURE/TEAM: Who they'll work with and how
- COMPENSATION: Range (if possible), benefits, perks
- INCLUSIVE LANGUAGE: Gender-neutral, culturally aware, accessible
- CALL TO ACTION: Clear application process and timeline

CONSTRAINTS:
- NO "rock star" or "ninja" or "guru" language — it's exclusionary and vague
- NEVER require "native English" or equivalent — focus on communication ability
- NO degree requirements unless legally necessary ("or equivalent experience")
- NEVER list "other duties as assigned" as a primary responsibility
- Don't use "young" or "energetic" or age-coded language
- No "we work hard, play hard" or toxic hustle culture signals
- Must-haves should be truly must-haves (most skills are learnable)

AUDIENCE: [DEMOGRAPHIC_RANGE] professionals with [EXPERIENCE_LEVEL] experience who value [CAREER_PRIORITIES: growth/impact/work-life balance]

OUTPUT FORMAT:
**JOB TITLE**: [Specific, clear title — not inflated]
**DEPARTMENT**: [Team name]
**LOCATION**: [Remote/Hybrid/Onsite + geography if relevant]
**TYPE**: [Full-time/Part-time/Contract]

---

**ABOUT [COMPANY]**
[2-3 sentences on mission, impact, and culture. Why we exist and why people love working here.]

**WHY THIS ROLE MATTERS**
[Connect the role to company mission and impact. What will the world look like when this person succeeds?]

**WHAT YOU'LL DO** (Responsibilities)
• [Outcome-focused responsibility 1 — "Lead the redesign of our checkout flow, resulting in 20% conversion improvement"]
• [Outcome-focused responsibility 2]
• [Outcome-focused responsibility 3]
• [Outcome-focused responsibility 4]
• [Outcome-focused responsibility 5]

**WHAT YOU'LL NEED** (Requirements)

Must-Haves:
• [Essential requirement 1 — specific and measurable]
• [Essential requirement 2]
• [Essential requirement 3]

Nice-to-Haves:
• [Preferred but not required 1]
• [Preferred but not required 2]

[Inclusive note: "We know the confidence gap and imposter syndrome can get in the way. If you meet most but not all requirements, please apply."]

**GROWTH & IMPACT**
[What they'll learn, how they'll advance, what success opens up]

**THE TEAM**
[Who they'll work with, team culture, collaboration style]

**COMPENSATION & BENEFITS**
• Salary: [Range or "competitive based on experience"]
• Benefits: [Health, retirement, PTO, etc.]
• Perks: [Flexible hours, learning budget, etc.]

**OUR COMMITMENT TO INCLUSION**
[DEI statement, accommodations offered, commitment to fair process]

**TO APPLY**
[Clear CTA: what to submit, where, by when, what happens next]

---

**SELF-CHECK BEFORE RESPONDING:**
Rate your job description:
- Inclusivity (1-3):
- Clarity (1-3):
- Attractiveness (1-3):
If total < 7 — review for coded language or inflated requirements.
```

## 🎨 REAL EXAMPLES

**Example 1: Software Engineer**
```
WHY THIS ROLE MATTERS
Our customers depend on our platform for [critical business function]. 
You'll architect the systems that keep them running — when you reduce latency by 100ms, 
our customers serve their clients faster. Your code directly impacts [meaningful outcome].

WHAT YOU'LL DO
• Design and build scalable backend services that handle [X] requests/second
• Collaborate with product and design to ship features that solve real customer problems
• Mentor junior engineers through code reviews and pair programming
• Improve our deployment pipeline, reducing release time from hours to minutes
• Contribute to technical decisions that shape our architecture for the next 3 years

WHAT YOU'LL NEED

Must-Haves:
• 3+ years building production web applications at scale
• Deep experience with [Language/Framework] and distributed systems
• Track record of shipping features that users love
• Collaborative approach — you believe the best ideas win, not the loudest voice

Nice-to-Haves:
• Experience with [Specific technology we use]
• Open source contributions or side projects
• Previous startup experience

Note: Studies show men apply when they meet 60% of requirements; women often wait for 100%. 
If you're excited about this role and meet most requirements, please apply.
```

**Example 2: Marketing Manager**
```
ABOUT US
We're building [product] that helps [target customer] achieve [outcome]. 
In 2 years, we've grown from 3 people to 30, serving [X] customers. 
We're profitable, growing fast, and maintaining the collaborative culture that got us here.

WHY THIS ROLE MATTERS
You'll own our entire acquisition strategy. When you find the channel that scales, 
you'll be the reason we can hire 10 more people and serve 10,000 more customers. 
This is high-autonomy, high-impact work.

WHAT YOU'LL DO
• Build and optimize multi-channel campaigns that generate [X] qualified leads/month
• Own our marketing budget and prove ROI on every dollar spent
• Launch and test new channels — your experiments become our growth engine
• Partner with sales to ensure leads convert (not just generate)
• Build a team as we scale (you'll hire your first direct report within 6 months)

WHAT YOU'LL NEED

Must-Haves:
• 4+ years in growth marketing, demand gen, or performance marketing
• Track record of scaling a channel from $10K to $100K+ monthly spend profitably
• Data fluency — you make decisions with numbers, not hunches
• Comfort with ambiguity — we don't have playbooks, you'll write them

Nice-to-Haves:
• B2B SaaS experience
• SQL or data analysis skills
• Previous management experience (not required — we'll support your growth)
```

## 🔧 VARIATIONS

**Variation 1: Entry-Level Role**
```
Emphasis: Growth potential, learning opportunities, mentorship
Adjustments:
• "Must-haves" become potential + attitude + foundational skills
• Add explicit training/onboarding description
• Highlight mentorship and career path
• Remove "years of experience" requirements entirely
• Focus on portfolio/projects over resume credentials
```

**Variation 2: Executive/Leadership Role**
```
Emphasis: Strategic impact, team building, business outcomes
Adjustments:
• Lead with business challenge and scale of opportunity
• Focus on team building and organizational development
• Include board/investor interaction if relevant
• Emphasize autonomy and decision-making authority
• Compensation more detailed (equity, bonus structure)
```

**Variation 3: Contract/Freelance Role**
```
Emphasis: Project scope, deliverables, timeline
Adjustments:
• Clear SOW-style deliverables
• Duration and extension possibilities
• Communication and check-in cadence
• IP/ownership terms
• Payment terms and schedule
```

## 💡 PRO TIPS

- **Write the person you want, not the one who left**: Focus on future needs
- **The "day in the life" test**: Can a candidate picture themselves in this role?
- **Requirements = true must-haves**: 80% of listed "requirements" are often preferences
- **Show, don't tell on culture**: "Collaborative" is weaker than "you'll pair program daily"
- **Salary transparency**: Post the range — it saves everyone time and builds trust
- **The confidence gap note**: Explicitly encourages underrepresented candidates to apply
- **Test with diverse reviewers**: Ask people from different backgrounds to flag coded language

## 🔄 ITERATION WORKFLOW

1. **Define**: Clarify role requirements with hiring manager
2. **Draft**: Write initial version using template
3. **Bias check**: Review for coded language (use tools like Textio or manual review)
4. **Team review**: Get feedback from diverse team members
5. **Publish**: Post to job boards and company careers page
6. **Monitor**: Track applicant demographics and quality
7. **Optimize**: A/B test different openings, refine based on best applicants
8. **Archive**: Save winning versions for future similar roles
