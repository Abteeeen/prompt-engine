- Response 2: Question + Isolate (diagnose real concern, narrow scope)
- Response 3: Story + Proof (similar situation, successful outcome)

- Include the EXACT words to say, not just concepts

- NEVER argue with the prospect or say "but"

- NO feature-comparison trash talking competitors

## 🎨 REAL EXAMPLES

**Example 1: Price Objection**
```
OBJECTION: "Your competitor is 30% cheaper"

RESPONSE 1 - Empathy Pivot:
"Yeah, [Competitor] makes sense if budget is the only filter. 
Most of our clients came from there — want to know what made them switch?"

RESPONSE 2 - Question Drill:
"If price wasn't a factor, what would be your top 3 must-haves? 
Let's see where we actually compare."

RESPONSE 3 - Story Proof:
"[Similar Company] went with the cheaper option initially. 
Six months later, migration costs ate their savings. 
What's your read on total cost of ownership?"
```

**Example 2: Timing Objection**
```
OBJECTION: "We need to wait until next quarter"

RESPONSE 1:
"Q3 starts in 6 weeks. What's the one thing that HAS to happen between now and then?"
[Listen] → "What happens if that slips?"

RESPONSE 2:
"Totally get budget cycles. The teams who start now are usually live before their Q3 starts. 
What's the cost of waiting vs. starting?"

PREVENTION:
"Before we talk numbers — when do you need to be live to hit [Goal]?"
[Establishes urgency before price discussion]
```

## 🔧 VARIATIONS

**Variation 1: Authority Objection**
```
"I need to run this by my boss"
→ Response: "Should we loop them in now, or do you need to prep them first?"
→ Prevention: "Who else should be in this conversation?"
```

**Variation 2: Competition Objection**
```
"We're also looking at [Competitor]"
→ Response: "What drew you to them? [Listen] → Here's where we differ..."
→ Prevention: Lead with unique differentiator early in pitch
```

**Variation 3: Feature Gap Objection**
```
"You don't have [Feature] that [Competitor] has"
→ Response: "You're right. What does that feature solve for you? 
[Listen] → Here's how our clients handle that..."
→ Prevention: Proactively address missing features before they ask
```

## ✅ QUALITY CHECKLIST

- [ ] Response acknowledges their concern before pivoting
- [ ] Reframe changes perspective without arguing
- [ ] At least one response asks a diagnostic question
- [ ] Social proof is specific (company name, metric, timeline)
- [ ] Prevention strategy addresses root cause
- [ ] Clear criteria for when to walk away

---

## 💡 PRO TIPS

- **The "Yes, and" technique**: Never contradict — always build on their reality
- **Use the "What would need to be true" frame**: Gets them imagining success
- **Objections are buying signals**: Silence after objection = they're processing
- **The 3-second rule**: Wait 3 full seconds after delivering reframe
- **Label and pause**: "It seems like [concern] is the real blocker here" → wait
- **Trade, don't discount**: If price is the issue, what can they offer in exchange?
