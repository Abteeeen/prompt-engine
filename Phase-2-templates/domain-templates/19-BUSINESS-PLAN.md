## 🎨 REAL EXAMPLES

**Example 1: Market Sizing**
```
2.2 MARKET SIZE

TAM (Total Addressable Market):
All businesses globally with remote teams needing project management software.
~$5.2B annually (Gartner, 2024)

SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market):
Mid-market companies (50-500 employees) in North America and Europe.
~$890M annually

SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market):
Our initial target: SaaS companies with 50-200 employees in US/Canada.
~$47M annually

Bottom-up validation:
• 12,000 target companies in SOM
• Average contract value: $15K/year
• 25% annual growth in remote work tools
• Our 3-year target: 250 customers = $3.75M ARR
```

**Example 2: Unit Economics**
```
4.3 UNIT ECONOMICS (SaaS Example)

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):
• Marketing spend per customer: $2,400
• Sales cost per customer: $1,600
• Total CAC: $4,000

Lifetime Value (LTV):
• Average revenue per customer: $15K/year
• Gross margin: 75%
• Annual churn: 10%
• Customer lifetime: 10 years
• LTV: $15K × 0.75 × 10 = $112,500

LTV:CAC Ratio: 28:1 (Exceptional — benchmark is 3:1)
Payback period: 3.2 months (Benchmark is 12-18 months)
```

## 🔧 VARIATIONS

**Variation 1: Internal Strategic Plan**
```
Focus: Team alignment, operational milestones, resource allocation
Add: OKRs, quarterly priorities, departmental budgets
Remove: Investor-specific sections, funding ask
Tone: Action-oriented, tactical
```

**Variation 2: Grant Application**
```
Focus: Social impact, innovation, sustainability, job creation
Add: Detailed budget breakdown, community benefit, environmental impact
Remove: High growth projections, exit strategy
Tone: Responsible, community-focused, compliant
```

**Variation 3: Bank Loan Application**
```
Focus: Cash flow stability, collateral, repayment ability
Add: Personal guarantees, asset lists, credit history
Remove: High-risk growth projections, equity discussions
Tone: Conservative, risk-averse, security-focused
```

## ✅ QUALITY CHECKLIST

- [ ] Executive summary stands alone (investors can decide from just this)
- [ ] Market sizing has both top-down and bottom-up validation
- [ ] Competitive analysis includes "do nothing" as an option
- [ ] Financial projections show key assumptions clearly
- [ ] Team section highlights relevant domain expertise
- [ ] "Why now" timing is clearly articulated
- [ ] Risks are honest and mitigations are credible
- [ ] Use of funds is specific (not "working capital and growth")
- [ ] Milestones are measurable and time-bound
- [ ] Writing is clear enough for non-industry readers

---

## 💡 PRO TIPS

- **Write the executive summary last**: But make it the strongest section
- **TAM/SAM/SOM progression**: Show you understand realistic market penetration
- **The "so what" test**: Every claim needs a supporting data point or specific example
- **Team section matters most for early-stage**: Highlight why YOU are the team to build this
- **Financial assumptions table**: List every major assumption so investors can stress-test
- **The question test**: Have someone read it and ask them what questions remain unanswered

## 🔄 ITERATION WORKFLOW

1. **First draft**: Get all ideas down without editing
2. **Data pass**: Add specific numbers, sources, and citations
3. **Red team review**: Have someone poke holes in every claim
4. **Investor feedback**: Share with friendly investors for input
5. **Polish**: Cut 20% of words — make every sentence earn its place
6. **Financial stress-test**: Model best/worst/base case scenarios
7. **Update cadence**: Quarterly updates with new traction/milestones
